From Me to You - A Series of Reccomendations

From period pieces to lightweight comedies, find your next liking here!

Historical Pieces

Bridgerton

Bridgerton is a period drama series set in early 19th-century Regency-era London, based on the book series by Julia Quinn. The show follows the wealthy and glamorous Bridgerton family, led by matriarch Violet Bridgerton, as they navigate the social intricacies, scandals, and romantic entanglements of London's elite during the Regency period.

The Buccaneers

The Buccaneers is a period drama series based on the novel by Edith Wharton. Set in the late 19th century, the show follows a group of young American women from wealthy families who travel to England in search of suitable husbands in British high society. The series explores themes of love, ambition, and social status as these American "dollar princesses" navigate the rigid class system of the British aristocracy.

The Crown

The Crown is a critically acclaimed historical drama series that chronicles the reign of Queen Elizabeth II, offering a deep, nuanced portrayal of the British royal family and the political and personal challenges the Queen faced during her time on the throne. The show spans decades, beginning in the 1940s with Elizabeth's unexpected ascent to the throne following the death of her father, King George VI, and covering significant events in British and world history through the 20th century.

The Gilded Age

The Gilded Age is a period drama series created by Julian Fellowes, set in the opulent and rapidly changing world of 1880s New York City. The show takes place during a time of enormous social, economic, and cultural upheaval, when the United States was emerging as a global power, and old social orders were being challenged by new money and industrial wealth.

Comedy Pieces

Modern Family

Modern Family is a mockumentary-style sitcom that revolves around the lives of three interconnected families, each with their own unique dynamics and quirks. The series centers on the Pritchett-Dunphy-Tucker clan, led by the patriarch Jay Pritchett, who is married to a much younger woman, Gloria, and has a son named Manny. Jay’s daughter, Claire, is married to Phil Dunphy, and they have three children: Haley, Alex, and Luke. Claire’s brother, Mitchell, is married to Cameron Tucker, and together they adopt a daughter, Lily.

Brooklyn Nine-Nine

Brooklyn Nine-Nine is a hilarious and heartwarming ensemble sitcom set in the fictional 99th precinct of the New York City Police Department. The show follows a diverse group of detectives led by the immature but highly skilled Jake Peralta (Andy Samberg), whose antics are often a source of frustration for his no-nonsense captain, Raymond Holt (Andre Braugher). Holt, a by-the-book, openly gay man who brings a stern, stoic demeanor to the precinct, is frequently paired with Jake’s more carefree and playful style.

Friends

Friends is a beloved American sitcom that centers around a close-knit group of six friends—Rachel, Ross, Monica, Chandler, Joey, and Phoebe—living in New York City. The series, which ran from 1994 to 2004, captures the ups and downs of adulthood, love, work, and family, all through the lens of these six characters as they navigate life's challenges while relying on each other for support, laughter, and companionship.

Young Sheldon

Young Sheldon is a prequel to the popular sitcom The Big Bang Theory, focusing on the childhood of Sheldon Cooper, the eccentric, socially awkward genius played by Jim Parsons in the original series. Set in the late 1980s and early 1990s, the show follows Sheldon (Iain Armitage) as a young prodigy growing up in the small town of Galveston, Texas, at the age of nine. Despite his exceptional intellect and love for science, Sheldon struggles to relate to his peers, who find his behavior strange and often difficult to understand.

Watcher's Choices

Never Have I Ever

Never Have I Ever is a coming-of-age comedy-drama series created by Mindy Kaling and Lang Fisher. The show centers around Devi Vishwakumar (Maitreyi Ramakrishnan), a 15-year-old Indian-American high school student living in suburban California. After the sudden death of her father, Devi’s life has been turned upside down, leaving her to grapple with grief, identity, and the pressures of adolescence while navigating the ups and downs of high school life.

Emily in Paris

Emily in Paris is a romantic comedy-drama series that follows Emily Cooper (Lily Collins), a young, ambitious marketing executive from Chicago who unexpectedly lands a job in Paris. When her company acquires a French luxury marketing firm, Emily is sent to the City of Light to bring an American perspective to the team. However, she quickly finds herself out of her depth, navigating cultural differences, workplace challenges, and the complexities of living in a foreign country.

Outer Banks

Outer Banks is an action-packed teen drama series set in the coastal town of the same name in North Carolina, where two distinct groups—the wealthy "Kooks" and the working-class "Pogues"—are locked in a bitter social divide. The show follows a group of Pogue teenagers led by John B. (Chase Stokes), a charismatic and adventurous boy who is determined to uncover the truth about his father's mysterious disappearance. John B. and his loyal friends—Kiara (Madison Bailey), Pope (Jonathan Daviss), and JJ (Rudy Pankow)—embark on a thrilling treasure hunt that leads them to a legendary sunken ship rumored to hold a vast fortune in gold.

The Summer I Turned Pretty

The Summer I Turned Pretty is a coming-of-age drama series based on the best-selling book trilogy by Jenny Han. The show follows the life of Isabel "Belly" Conklin (Lola Tung), a teenage girl who spends every summer at a beach house in Cousins Beach with her mother, Laurel, and her mother’s best friends, Susannah and her two sons, Conrad and Jeremiah Fisher.
